📄 Case Study: Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where individuals are targeted through social media 
platforms, messaging apps, or online forums. A common scenario involves a college student who 
becomes the target of repeated negative comments and memes posted by classmates on a social media
platform. Initially, the bullying may begin as jokes, but it gradually escalates into personal 
attacks, body shaming, and spreading false rumors.
In this case, the victim starts receiving abusive messages daily, and fake accounts are created
to mock and humiliate them publicly. As a result, the student feels stressed, anxious, and avoids 
attending college or participating in online activities. The situation worsens when private photos
are shared without consent, damaging the student’s reputation and confidence.
Cyberbullying typically happens due to anonymity, peer pressure, or lack of awareness about
consequences. Students and teenagers are the most common targets, as they are highly active online.
  The consequences can be serious, including mental health issues, academic decline, and loss of
self-esteem.
This case highlights the importance of responsible online behavior and strict action against 
offenders. Awareness and timely reporting can help prevent such incidents and create a safer 
digital environment for students.
🛡️ Prevention Checklist: Stay Safe from Cyberbullying
-Do not respond to abusive messages – Ignoring bullies reduces their power.
-Block and report the offender on the platform immediately.
-Keep your accounts private to limit access from unknown users.
-Avoid sharing personal information or photos publicly.
-Save evidence (screenshots of messages/posts) for reporting.
-Talk to a trusted person (friend, parent, teacher) if targeted.
-Use official reporting channels like cybercrime.gov.in.
-Call helpline 1930 in case of serious threats or harassment.
